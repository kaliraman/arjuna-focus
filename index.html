Eye ofthe Fish

Strike where the eye will be — not where it is.

Your arrow takes a moment. Lead the eye.

How to play

  • Aim — move your pointer (or drag on touch) to choose where to shoot.
  • Lead the eye — your arrow takes a moment to fly, and the eye keeps moving. Fire where the eye is about to be, so the arrow meets it.
  • The pupil is the bullseye. Land your arrow on it for a perfect strike.
  • Levels grow harder: the eye spins faster, so you must lead it further — amid more distraction.

The legend

In the Mahabharata, the master Drona hung a wooden fish high in a tree and bade each student strike its eye — looking only at its reflection in the pool below. One by one they spoke of the sky, the branches, the rippling water. Only Arjuna said: “I see the eye of the fish, and nothing else.”

Excess is the enemy of mastery. See only the eye.
