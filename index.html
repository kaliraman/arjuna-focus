Arjuna'sFocus

Drona hung a wooden fish high in a tree and bade each student strike its eye — looking only at its reflection in the pool below. One by one they spoke of the sky, the branches, the rippling water. Only Arjuna said: “I see the eye of the fish, and nothing else.”

Excess is the enemy of mastery. See only the eye.

How to play

  • Aim — move your pointer (or drag on touch) to guide the reticle over the pool.
  • Loose — click or tap to fire at the fish's eye in the reflection.
  • The pupil is the bullseye. The closer to it — and the faster your shot — the more you score.
  • Hold steady to build Focus; a calm eye stills the ripples and clears the water.
  • Levels grow harder: the fish spins faster and the pool fills with distraction until the eye shows for only an instant.
